Some facts about Agartala

  • Agartala /əɡərtəˈlɑː/ (Agortola) is the capital of Indian state of Tripura
  • It is the second largest city in North-east India after Guwahati, both in municipal area and population
  • The city is the seat of the Government of Tripura
  • Agartala is one of the fastest developing cities of India.
  • Agartala is a component of two words, namely "Agar", a kind of oily valuable perfume tree, + suffix "tala", a store house
  • The city is governed by the Agartala Municipal Corporation
  • Agartala lies on the bank of the Haora River and is located 2 km from the Bangladesh Border
  • Agartala is India's third international internet gateway after the ones in Mumbai and Chennai
  • Climate here is generally on the warmer side with a little bit of of humidity
  • Tripura is the only state in India which is ruled by the Communist Party of India (Marxist) CPI(M)
  • People here are mainly one of the two ethnicities: Bengalis and Tripuris
  • Having bengalis means the city is closer in culture to the city of Kolkata than rest of the North-East India
  • People here prefer going to Kolkata to get facilities that are available in nearest major city
  • The city and its people are is closer to the mother earth than many other modern cities in India
